300-YEAR STALEMATE ON BECCIDES BROKEN

From: beardog2150

After a grinding stalemate that has seen hundreds of millions dead and the Imperial Tithe go underpaid, the stalemate on the hive world of Beccides has at last been broken by Imperial forces! A specially-assembled assault force staged a bold landing far behind the front lines, delivering the brave soldiers of the Imperial Guard into the enemy's rear lines so that they can deliver the Emperor's justice!

Beccides, of course, is one of the great industrial worlds of the sector, and has been riven by strife ever since an unclear pronoun in paragraph 1,387 of the world's constitution caused a division over who should become the new planetary governor. With half of the planet's hives declaring for one candidate and the other half declaring for their sister, the interim government pled for Imperial clarification. When an official Administratum verdict was levied, the unsatisfied side immediately graduated to full rebellion! While the loyalists have continued to attempt to pay their tithe of precious combat knives, the conflict has been evaluated as 'low priority' due to the continued (albeit reduced) tithing, and the resultant deployment of Imperial forces has managed to contain the rebels, leading to the construction of a 100-mile deep system of fortifications between the factions.

When the brilliant Lord General Militant Zeromus ascended to command of the campaign, he selected a starport distant from rebel defense laser emplacements and unused in decades, striking with a carefully gathered hoard of planetary assault specialists , followed by a landing of highly mobile regiments from Armageddon, Krieg, and Savlar, able to thrive in the industrial wasteland and strike out rapidly! With a spaceport secured and Imperial regiments redeploying, it is estimated the war will be over in no more than fifty years! May the Emperor's benediction shine upon Lord General Militant Zeromus and the valiant loyalists of Beccides!

LAYOFFS

From: mourhurst

Holst, Virex, and Bastion Consortium ships sit idle in orbit or half-loaded in irradiated ports, accruing debt by the hour. Insurance bonds issued under Administratum writs have been frozen pending “planetary stabilization,” a phrase that in practice means nothing is paid and no timelines are given. Several minor houses have already defaulted, their assets quietly absorbed by larger guilds or impounded outright by planetary authorities. More damaging still is the erosion of trust. Offworld partners, once content to wait through Lamia Prime’s habitual delays, have begun rerouting convoys permanently. Sector logisticians now classify Lamian goods as “high-risk,” demanding prepayment and punitive collateral. The Trade Guilds’ greatest asset, that being reliability built over generations, has evaporated in a matter of months.

Many companies have laid off tens of millions of dockworkers, almost instantly guarantee unrest, Governor-Marshal Voss’s administration has urged patience and “temporary austerity,” offering no direct financial relief. Behind the scenes, the Departmento Munitorum has already moved to assert priority claims on remaining Guild assets, citing wartime necessity to the Sevran Dominate front at large and to requisition the unused ports as refuel site, using the planet’s Tithe. There are also talks of stationing more Arbites reinforcements in the planet as a preemptive measure for the upcoming riots that are predicted to come.

Rogue Planet caught in the HOGS EX System

From: lux3791

A small planet the size of a large dwarf planet was recently catched by the gravity well of the HOGS EX system. The planet is noted to be rich in "precious minerals" as said by Miner Superior Ka L. who is in charge of one of the many mining operations on the new planet.

Of special note is the high concentration of gold and a newly discovered mineral native to the planet, which has the size of two smaller dwarf planets. This brightly red colored mineral is said to be of use for multiple use cases.

As of now only surface level work was done, with Miner Superior Ka L. being the head of the first deep mining operation to be conducted. He told the Imperial Times: "It's nice that this rock doesn't seem to have any life on it. Nothing but minerals, rock and stone. It makes things much easier."

Interview with a Guardsman

From: paladintakezo

Unknown: “So, can you start us off by introducing yourself?”

Sgt. A. Sobiech: “Ambroźy Sobiech, 113-75-6408, Sergeant of the 3rd Platoon, 5th Airborne Tank Company, 201st Winged Hussars.”

Unknown: “Just your name would have sufficed, Sergeant, but thank you all the same. The full monty gives the whole thing a bit of credence. Could you give the listeners at home a rundown of your actions at Roane's Oak?”

Sgt. A. Sobiech: “Yes Comm…aye, sir. The 5th Airborne Tanks were given orders to support the Thunder Runners as they made the main push, really just ensuring no bleedin’ tyłek popped up with a heavy gun and put a hole in something important.”

Unknown: “I see, and did your 3rd Platoon have any luck with stopping any ‘bleedin’ tyłeks?’”

Sgt. A. Sobiech: “Aye, sir, a couple. The first handful were but a handful of stragglers, isolated in foxholes and trenches. Lesson no. 17 of the Karokand Maneuver Center of Perfection demands to always conserve ammo when possible, so we simply hit ‘em with the ol' bumper. They began to thicken up once we got closer to the line, but it was nothing a few rips of the autocannon couldn't handle.”

Unknown: “Excellent work, Sergeant. Could you expand on this ‘Karokand Maneuver Center?’ I doubt our listeners back home have ever heard of such an institution.”

Sgt. A. Sobiech: “Of course, sir, of course. The Karokand Maneuver Center of Perfection is the prim…the prem…the top tankin’ school on all of Karokand, and probably in the whole system. They taught us how to drive, how to load, how to shoot, all that good stuff, and for a bunch of different tanks too! I tell you, I might be in the back of a Chimera now, sir, but I was quite the marksman with a battlecannon back in the day!”

Unknown: “I believe it Sergeant, I do. Say, would you recommend this ‘Center’ for others? Has it made an improvement in your life?”

Sgt. A. Sobiech: “Oh, no doubt, sir! Why, before I joined up with the Hussars I was a bleedin’ snipe, a cut-purse! Gówno, I probably shouldn't say it, but I was no better than the tyłeks we ran over today. MCoP though, it gave me something. Discipline, respect…food…money. Joining the Hussars was the best thing to ever happen to me, and I thank the God-Emperor every day that he allows me to serve him!”

Unknown: “Thank you, Sergeant Sobiech, I think you've handled this interview well, and not to mention gave an astounding recommendation for the Karokand Maneuver…”

Sgt. A. Sobiech: “...and the women! Got to beat ‘em off with a stick, I say! Something about a man in uniform…”
