PROFILES IN COURAGE: IMPERIAL GUARD CORPORAL LOBO GILETINE OF ARMAGEDDON

From: beardog2150

The men and women of the Imperial Guard are the Imperium's sword and shield, fighting across a thousand thousand worlds for all of the loyal followers of the Emperor. Everyone, from the Lord Commander Militant, down to the lowest private, is a vital part of the Emperor's army, putting their bodies between the Imperial citizenry and the myriad terrors in the dark. Corporal Lobo Giletine is one such warrior, and having spent an hour with him, this reporter feels much safer.

Corporal Giletine hails from Armageddon, the famed hive world and home to the Steel Legion, the proud specialists in mechanized warfare. The warriors of Armageddon are from hardy stock, laboring day and night in the great workshops and factorums of the hives, churning out not only weapons to defend their own world, but those of the Imperium. Corporal Giletine is a most unusual example of this stock - pale and chem-scarred, as they often are, but standing well over six feet tall, with intense eyes; the most immediately apparent aspect of him, though, is his right arm, an industrial augmetic tipped in a pair of foot-long metal shears, able to cleave flesh and bone like a man with a knife carves butter.

The Corporal shared with me some of his history, from his time as a pit fighter in the underhive, cheering the spirits of his fellow man, to winning his freedom and battling the green menace out on the ash dunes, only to be saved by the man he still follows into battle. Since that day, he has fought xenos and renegades on multiple worlds, defending his fellow man and ending many a life with a swift snip. But of course it's not all battles and bloodshed; with the men of the Imperial Guard, Lobo has found comrades and friendship, and in service to the Emperor, fulfillment. Truly he stands as one of our boldest bulwarks, and an inspiration to any who may be wishing to take up the lasgun and defend their fellow man!

The Art of Making Good Corpse-Starch

From: mourhurst

By any measure of civilized dining, corpse-starch is not a cuisine. It is necessity rendered into brick form, the final courtesy of the Imperium to ensure that nothing given in service is wasted. Yet among the countless forge worlds and siege lines of the Astra Militarum, there persists a truth. Some corpse-starch are better than others. On worlds long accustomed to sieges, the preparation of corpse-starch has become a craft rather than a mere industrial process. Veterans will tell you that while the raw ingredients are uniform by Munitorum decree, the method to make your own corpse-starch makes all the difference.

The foundation is in the purity of the flesh. Proper corpse-starch begins with thorough rendering. All usable organic matter should be reduced, sterilized, and filtered until no trace of its former identity remains. Texture follows next. Experienced processors allow the rendered mass to settle before compression, preventing the chalk-dry consistency that breaks teeth. A slower press yields a denser, more uniform block. Seasoning, when or where permitted, is applied sparingly. Salt reclaimed from industrial runoff, trace mineral ash from promethium furnaces, or pulverized ration herbs are folded in during the cooling phase. None of this changes what corpse-starch is, but it changes how it tastes. Finally comes the cut. Properly cured corpse-starch is portioned into manageable slabs, each stamped and logged, then stored away from moisture and light. A well made block will keep for years and break cleanly when struck. It will taste significantly better than the munitorum issued corpse-starch cans.

Alternatively, you could just give the corpse-starch to a Ratling and they’ll figure it out.

From: .rayja

Interview with a Guardsman

From: paladintakezo

Lords, Ladies, and Loyal Servants of the God Emperor of Mankind,

Imperial Tactician Jan Łozowsk cordially invites you to join the celebration as Impetus celebrates its very first Regimental Founding! Festivities shall last well over a week, with the entire populace exempt from normal production quotas, but the culmination of the event will be the Grand Founding Parade! The Parade will feature Guardsmen and Astartes from multiple systems and worlds, all gathered on Impetus to showcase the glory and splendor of the Imperium, reaching its climax with the unveiling of the Impetan 1st Grenadiers!

The ceremony will begin with a blessing of our troopers by Rykus Prex, the Cardinal of Aquiline IV who has found time to honor us with his presence despite his ongoing pilgrimage. The procession will then begin, starting with the fearsome Karokand Hussars and ending with a speech from the commanding officer of the Impetan 1st, Colonel Voight.

After the parade has drawn to a close, dignitaries and military leaders will be invited for a night of refreshments and relaxation at the manse of Duchess Anke Tanner-Rausch, Planetary Governor of all Impetus. A formal dress-code has been implemented, but concessions can be made for those who lacked the foresight to pack for social functions when shipping off to war.

Open slots in the Grand Founding Parade's Itinerary are swiftly drawing to a close, so make sure to reserve yours today!
