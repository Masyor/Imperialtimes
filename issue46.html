NEW GENERAL LEADS IMPERIAL EXPEDITION INTO THE VINTA SUBSECTOR

From: mourhurst

In a move signaling renewed Imperial confidence in the reclamation of lost worlds, Major General Dimitry Vladislek Maxim, decorated veteran of the Vostroyan 578th Firstborn Support Division, has been elevated to the temporary rank of Command Marshal and entrusted with leadership of the Baritone Expedition inti the vastly unknown Subsector of Vinta.

The appointment, formally endorsed by Lord General Wilhelmi and several unnamed Inquisitor Lords, places Maxim as the overall commander of reclaiming and claiming planets in the name of the God-Emperor. The force is tasked to additionally purge the region which is plagued by xenos infestation and abominable intelligence. The latter foe has attracted a nearby Archmagos by the name of Macrotek Gelvan to volunteer for the expedition specifically to purge the machine beasts of the Vinta Subsector. As for honorable mentions, the regiments of Krieg have arrived to assist.

The expedition is also backed by substantial funding from an unknown sibling of Rogue Trader Deliria for their own personal reasons. In addition, the planetary governors of Vinta and Todr have pledged regiments and voidships to bolster the effort. And to add onto all that, Brother-Sergeant Benkei Mushin of the White Scars Chapter has also pledged his support in hunting the enemies of man out of the subsector.

Officers within the expedition prior to warp jumping, expressed overwhelming confidence in the success of such a combined force. Describing the undertaking as a chance to bring the Emperor’s light once more to the mostly unknown void of the subsector. The Exacta Departmento meanwhile expresses its delight at the opportunity to safely collect the tithes of outposts within the subsector that are long overdue with the aide of the Expeditionary Force. As well as setting the tithe grade of the new planets this expedition will colonize in the name of the God-Emperor.

WINGS OF FIRE: AN INTERVIEW WITH COMMANDER ÁSLAUG ‘VALKYRIE’ RANDAHLÍN

From: phoenix_actual

The woman before me is even taller than I imagined. For a name whispered across the Guard forces of the sector with near-mythic awe, Commander Áslaug “Valkyrie” Randahlín is remarkably human. Her steely blue eyes carry the distant focus of one who’s lived too long amid flak fire and falling ash. Her bionics are scratched and scarred, and her carapace plating is painted with Valkyrie unit markings and other Brinstar designs - not sanctioned, though none would dare ask.

She greets me with a smile - tight, professional, but undeniably tired. For those unacquainted with her legend, Commander Randahlín leads the 4th Brinstar Valkyries, a Storm Trooper regiment famous - or infamous - for its unconventional airborne tactics and fierce independence. These qualities have earned them both admiration and scrutiny within the Munitorum.

When asked about her regiment’s reputation for unorthodox methods and discipline issues, Áslaug’s smirk widens slightly. “We fight how we must. The Emperor doesn’t care how we win, only that we do.” Her words are blunt, bordering on heretical pragmatism, but there’s a weight behind them. Those who serve under her describe her as fearless, relentless, and utterly devoted to her troops. The Valkyries revere her not just as a commander, but as one of their own - a leader who shares their risks, their vices, and their victories.

When pressed about her callsign ,“Valkyrie”, she laughs. “It was given to me by the troops,” she says. “I guess it started as some sort of way to let me know I was one of them.”

Throughout the interview, it becomes clear that Randahlín’s strength lies not in protocol or doctrine, but in her belief in motion - that stagnation is death, that survival depends on striking first, hardest, and from above. “We don’t wait for permission to act,” she adds quietly, almost to herself. “We make our own openings.”

When I ask what drives her; glory, duty, faith, she doesn’t answer at first. Instead, she looks out the hangar viewport where Phoenix Squadron’s gunships rest, their hulls scarred and blackened. Finally, she says:

“We fight because we must. Glory fades, faith wavers, but the next battle always comes. And when it does, we’ll always be ready.”
