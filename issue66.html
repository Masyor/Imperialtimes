Horrors of the Marsh

From: mourhurst

In accordance with standing Munitorum procedure, the following account summarizes an irregular event observed during routine garrison operations at Juniper Σ24 Relay Station. The report is submitted for informational awareness due to the potential presence of unknown archaeological materials within Imperial installations.

On the third night following the artillery exchange along the Kinesline Front of the Marsh, the station vox array began receiving an unidentified transmission on multiple frequencies simultaneously. Initial analysis suggested atmospheric interference caused by lingering bombardment residue. However, the signal demonstrated coherent structure and repeated a phrase in both High and Low Gothic before the system was manually reset. Soldiers of the 42nd were sent down to investigate.

The exact wording has been removed from this summary due to the possibility of misinterpretation by untrained personnel.

Return what was taken from the vault

Below the stone floor we uncovered a hidden chamber that was not listed on any architectural schematic. The construction style was markedly older than the rest of the station. Whoever built the chamber had done so before the relay complex even existed. The doorway bore an Imperial Aquila, however the eagle’s eyes were closed. Its wings were wrapped in chains. Inside the chamber stood a containment lattice surrounding a single suspended object. The object was a sphere of black crystalline material roughly the size of a human heart. It did not reflect light. Thin veins of violet light moved slowly within the stone like blood beneath skin. The moment the vault door opened, the vox-net activated again without power.

“You still keep the prisoner on Terra.”

Captain Elbrek immediately ordered the chamber resealed and declared the object forbidden until the Adeptus Mechanicus could examine it.

The station astropath, who had collapsed earlier when the signal first appeared, rose from the infirmary cot and began speaking in a voice that was not his own. “It devours HIM! It sustains HIM! The shadow behind the Throne! The crown waiting in the Dark! THEY WAIT! THEY LISTEN!” The rest of the words were promptly cut short when the Commissar arrived to execute the Astropath.

At the same time, sentries on the perimeter began reporting movement in the marsh fog. At first the shapes appeared to be infantry formations gathering in no man’s land. Closer observation revealed something impossible. The figures in the fog were soldiers of the 42nd Regiment. Not living soldiers, but the dead, reported by the sentries. According to officers, they were troopers who had fallen during the campaign days earlier. Lasfire proved to be ineffective, but salvation was soon to be achieved when members of the Sisters of Battle present found their destruction.

Two days later, representatives of the Adeptus Mechanicus arrived to secure the unidentified archaeological device discovered beneath the relay chapel. The object was removed for proper examination and containment according to standard relic-handling procedure. The relay station has since returned to full operational status, and morale among the garrison remains exemplary. The conduct of the 42nd Regiment and the Sisters of Battle present during the disturbance has been formally commended by sector command.

Citizens of the Imperium should therefore take heart. Even in the darkest marsh, even where strange whispers drift through the fog, the Emperor’s light remains unbroken. Wherever His faithful stand watch, the rchenemy can never win.

Securing the Void Lanes of Tartur

From: colonelgorgon

The 13th Polanskian Battlegroup and 68th Armageddon conducted a multi-month operation of Naval Defense. Keeping evacuation lanes open. Both formation's leadership showed excellent naval tactical acumen. The 13th's highlight was boarding a Chaos Battleship capturing it and sending it on a Collision Course with a Chaos Space Hulk.

The 68th showed excellent use of Aeronautica assets and even coming into direct combat with its Battleship which is dedicated for Aeronautical campaigns, at great risk to ship and self to prevent utter destruction of the 13th. Beating back multiple Chaos attacks before Navis Imperialis High Command ordered their withdrawal from the Campaign due to excessive cost of loss being risked by massive warp storms that wreaked havoc in space and on planet.

The 13th and 68th with great courage ensured nearly 85% of the planet could evacuate. Leaving the rest to hopefully pull out on their own accord. Will the guard be able to get the rest out and if so will they be able to withdraw to save resources to fight on later. Only time will tell.

THE EMPEROR'S BLESSING PROTECTS IMPERIAL PROFITS

From: beardog2150

Truly, the Emperor's eye sees all, and his touch can be felt all across the galaxy! A Squat merchant vessel, traveling to the forge world of Phaistea in Hacyria sector, has reported a most unusual instance during its travels: the sight of a new debris field in the Gnoria R18 system, a debris field made up of the ruins of many pirate voidships!

As we had previously reported, the new trade agreement between several Imperial entities and the Squat League of Grungni has been signed, bringing the surety of prosperity to all involved! Of course the future Rogue Trader, Joao d'Ordonha, is awaiting the arrival of his new Warrant of Trade, as well as the transfer of a Naval vessel to his command. Our impetuous abhuman allies, the League of Grungni, has seen no need to wait to fulfill their side of the bargain, and already their first void transport has made its way from Squat space, laden with precious materials for the red-robed priests of Mars!

Of course, evil is a constant threat to the loyal servants of the Emperor, and somehow, multiple pirate gangs acquired knowledge of the Squat vessel's route. We can all rest assured that no doubt the Adeptus Arbites and other enforcement entities will be investigating what Imperial servant would be so depraved as to sell out our abhuman cousins for some paltry profit, albeit at the cost of staining their soul; perhaps, of course, it was foul sorcery! In any case, though, the Emperor never sleeps, and once these renegades arrived at their destination, they were shocked to find each other. Whether they attempted to make common cause or not, we will never know, because in the end, they fell to fighting each other like a pack of starving neojackals, leaving the way for the merchantman clear!

Seven Houses, One Will: The Glorious Defense of Fuldera

From: super_mario.

What is more honorable than defending those weaker than you. What is more glorious than destroying a foe greater than you. The Knight houses battling on the surface of Fuldera know both these qualities well, for they have battled against a Green Tide so massive it was visible from the space above the world. Yet, this is not a story void of hope and heart. For over half a dozen Knightly Houses stood against the Green Tide, honorable and vigilant all of them. Over a dozen engines deployed against the green xenos, the orks were unable to find any true footing amongst the agriworld, unable to pierce the defenses of House Karel and their allied houses in this endeavor.

Yet, the Orks, ever treacherous in the face of certain defeat, brought together scrap and dung to bring together an absolute monstrosity, a mockery of all feats of engineering and of aesthetic design.

As the Knights of House Falcarius, House Dakrar, House Artair, House Aurelin, House Faring, House Presa, and House Karel slaughtered the greenskin invaders, the xenos raised their unholy abomination from scrap and xenos magic. The Knights defending the Capital were nearly caught unaware if not for the heroic actions of Knight Scion Robar Falcarius, Wielder of Brightclaw and soon to be savior of the Capital. For the Monstrosity attempted to bring down Brightroar, but with a triumphant and wonderful display of endurance the Knight took the shot and only began to fight even more.

Through great effort and coordination, only achievable by the Honorable Knight Houses, the great scrap beast was brought to its knees. A resounding victory over that of the Xenos, what was left of the greenskin horde turned to flight. The cowardly xenos fail to live and fight, only to die and run. The allied forces of the Knightly houses pursed, pressing against the bulk of greenskin infantry until finally the horde broke into a route, a resounding cheer going up through the Capital as the warriors of Fuldera began to chase after the broken orkish horde, intending to put an end to this threat once and for all.

Heretics Squashed!

From: enmera

A profane group of heretics on the eastern fringes of Segmentum Ultima has been recently put down by the Adeptus Arbites. Many reports on the situation have claimed that these heretics were rebelling over heretical ideals likely instilled in them by the seditious Tau. The heretics arose in protest of a recent campaign in a neighboring system, and official records state that the efforts of these heretics are most likely a last ditch effort by the xenos to instill some sort of damage. The campaign in question has been ongoing for approximately a decade now. The Imperial Guard has done well to hold the planet and resist the efforts of the foul enemy, but it has been a long and grueling fight, with much blood having been spilt. The Astra Militarum has recently retaken the capital, and with rumors going around that they will be maybe deploying to other worlds in the sector, heretics have began to show their true colors. The Adeptus Arbites says that, for now, the situation remains under control.
