CHAOS UNFOLDS AT THE IMPERIAL TIMES!

From: someguui

After the Festivities of Sanguinala, Tragedy struck within the Imperial Times Office when an Eversor of the Officio Assassinorum was released within the offices particularly on the floor reporting on Calixis Sector. The Eversor was released and active for 37 Terran Hours and Allegedly was Screaming "YOU ARE FAKE NEWS" until a second Eversor assassin was released to eliminate the first. While the First Assassin was eliminated and the second recalled promptly a large section of the Imperial Times staff had been butchered.

The Grand Provost Marshal has ordered an investigation on the matter and has determined that the First Eversor was smuggled in by the aide of an insider within the Imperial Times however due to the secrecy of the Officio Assassinorum the investigation has come to a administrative halt. The Imperial Times would like to issue an apology to all its readers within or interested in the Calixis Sector as we experience difficulties reporting on the sector.

FOOD DELIVERIES RESUME AFTER LONG MONTHS OF RATIONING

From: mourhurst

For the first time in 11 months, the markets of Lamia Prime have seen food again. Actual food. At first bell this cycle, bulk landers from Agri-World Ceres-Delta broke atmosphere under full escort, their holds heavy with grain, root-vegetables and vat-grown proteins. The Departmento Munitorum confirmed that forced rationing, enacted during the height of the supply interdiction, has been formally lifted for all non-frontline districts. Standard starch blocks and nutrient pastes will remain in reserve, but food allocations will now be distributed weekly through sanctioned channels with minor Hospitaller Orders being involved to aide this. And the first Militant Orders have also arrived to lay waste to the heretics that caused this rebellion. Administratum pict-feeds captured scenes of families standing patiently in the cold, children staring at crates of produce as if they were a great relic. Morale has never been higher. As the issue of malnutrition is being slowly fixed, an increase in PDF numbers shows well for the war effort.

In related developments, officials confirm that approximately half of Lamia Prime remains stable and compliant, maintaining sufficient output to meet its Imperial Tithe even amid open rebellion. The planet’s role as a supply hub endures, continuing to provide provisions to the greater frontline against the Dominate. Additional Imperial Guard regiments are en route, tasked with crushing remaining resistance so that loyal sectors are no longer strained in their sacred obligations.

CONTEST RESULTS RELEASED AMID SMOKING RUINS: WAS THE BLOODSHED WORTH IT?

Following weeks of administrative paralysis, the intervention of "efficiency consultants," and three months of bloody street-to-street fighting in the Archival District, the Archival Adjudication Subcommittee (AASS-SC) has finally released the winner of the Most Morbid and Prophetically Grimdark Deployment Document contest.

However, as the smoke clears from the barricades and the tally of casualties reaches the tens of thousands, a hushed question echoes through the hab-blocks: Was it worth it? While the winning documents were of undispitably high quality, critics point out that the riots destroyed three major ration-processing plants and the very archive meant to store the documents. The High Provost has declared the event a "resounding success for Imperial morale," though he issued this statement from an armored bunker.

The winning documents are available for viewing, though citizens are reminded that weeping in public remains a finable offense.
